It's nice to meet you.

As a true child of the 80’s, my path as a designer began with Pee-Wee Herman, Lisa Frank, Trapper Keepers, Swatch watches and the Double Dare obstacle course. I now know that this common aesthetic was inspired by the Memphis Design Group, but at the time I was innately drawn to the bright, bold and happy colors and shapes.

Not too long ago, I moved back to my hometown of Cleveland from Los Angeles where I held administrative positions in a market research firm and a movie studio, among other places. Looking back, it was the design-related tasks that I found most satisfying. Invitations to the Employee Appreciation Party, company-wide emails announcing the weekly fridge clean-out, and the client holiday cards were thoughtfully detailed, unexpectedly humorous, and exceedingly well-received. I also took pleasure in streamlining processes so that my bosses and co-workers would actually enjoy situations and tasks that were once avoided. Even so, I came to a point where I knew it was time to re-evaluate my career path.

UX design involves what I liked so much about my previous jobs and builds off of inherent characteristics of my personality that have been gestating since my childhood. In addition to being highly empathetic and a keen observer, I am eager to be in a position where I can make a real impact on people’s daily experiences and interactions.

It is my aim as a designer to bring joy and ease (and sometimes humor) into everyday tasks. I’m inspired by this quote from one of my design heroes,

“When I was young, all we ever heard about was
functionalism, functionalism, functionalism.
It’s not enough. Design should also be sensual and exciting.”
-Ettore Sottsass
